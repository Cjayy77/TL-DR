Gaze Feature: Regression Rate
The Regression Paragraph
This paragraph contains deliberate errors you will notice mid-sentence, forcing your eyes to move backwards to re-read. Watch the debug dot jump rightward-to-leftward repeatedly.
target: regression_rate > 0.25 → confused state

The capital of Australia is Sydney — wait, that is incorrect. The capital is Canberra, not Sydney, which is the largest city. Many readers aceept this misstake without noticing — did you spot the two spleling errors in that sentence? Go back and re-raed it. You will find "aceept" and "spleling" both contain transopsed letters, and "re-raed" is itself a deliberate error. Reading researchers call this the misspelling detection effect: the eye moves forward, the brain detects an anomaly, and the eye regresses to the point of confusion before continuing. This backwards movement — a saccade in the rightward-to-leftward direction — is precisely what TL;DR measures as regression rate.

Teh sentence you are raeding now contains tow erros at teh start and one in teh middle. Your eye has almost certainly jumped backwards at least twice already. A reader with no comprehension difficulty reads forward ninety percent of the time. A confused or error-detecting reader may make backwards movements on thirty to fifty percent of their saccades. TL;DR treats a regression rate above twenty percent sustained over two and a half seconds — combined with near-zero scroll — as a signal that the reader needs assistance.

What TL;DR should do here: detect high regression rate, classify as confused, fetch an explanation of whichever paragraph your gaze was on when the threshold was crossed. Use Alt+1 to simulate this immediately.
Classifier State: Confused
The Confusion Paragraph
Dense, unfamiliar terminology presented without definition. Your fixation durations will increase, saccades will shorten, and scroll will stop.
target: avg_fixation_ms > 380 + scroll_delta ≈ 0 → confused state

The epistemological implications of supervenience relations between qualia and neurophysiological substrates remain contentious within philosophy of mind. Proponents of non-reductive physicalism argue that phenomenal consciousness supervenes nomologically but not logically on physical states, implying the conceivability of epiphenomenal zombies as a rebuke to type-identity theory. Contrariwise, eliminativist accounts following Churchland and Dennett hold that folk-psychological intentional states constitute a degenerative research programme whose taxonomic categories fail to carve nature at its joints, rendering the explanatory gap a pseudo-problem generated by category error rather than genuine ontological discontinuity between the phenomenal and the physical.

The preceding paragraph was deliberately written at maximum Flesch-Kincaid difficulty. Every term — supervenience, qualia, epiphenomenal, eliminativist, folk-psychological, intentional states — requires prior domain knowledge to decode. If you do not have that knowledge, your fixations become long dwelling attempts to extract meaning, your saccades shorten because you cannot advance until you understand the current word, and your scroll position does not change because you are stuck. This is precisely the gaze signature of the confused state.

What TL;DR should do here: detect long fixations, short saccades, zero scroll, classify as confused, and offer an explanation of the dense paragraph above. Use Alt+1 to trigger this immediately.
Classifier State: Zoning Out
The Zoning Out Paragraph
Deliberately repetitive, monotonous content that causes the mind to disengage while the eyes continue their mechanical forward movement.
target: avg_fixation_ms > 700 + gaze_drift_px > 60 → zoning_out state

The meeting was scheduled for Tuesday. On Tuesday the meeting took place. Everyone who had been invited to Tuesday's meeting attended the meeting that was scheduled. The meeting lasted approximately as long as meetings of this type typically last. After the meeting concluded, the people who had attended the meeting returned to the places they had been before the meeting. A summary of the meeting was later distributed to the people who had attended the meeting, as is customary following meetings of this nature. The next meeting was then scheduled for the following Tuesday.

The passage above contains nothing that requires active comprehension. The words are simple, the sentences are short, the content is completely devoid of information. When the brain detects this, it disengages from active processing. The eyes continue moving across the lines — making their mechanical saccades — but they begin to drift vertically off the text baseline. Fixations become very long not because the words are hard but because the brain is not processing them at all. Scroll slows to nearly zero because nothing is motivating forward progress.

What TL;DR should do here: detect very long fixations, high vertical gaze drift, zero scroll, classify as zoning out, and pulse a green highlight on the current paragraph. No AI call — just a gentle visual nudge back to attention. Use Alt+3 to simulate this immediately.
Classifier State: Overloaded
The Overload Paragraph
Multiple concepts introduced simultaneously with dependencies between them, triggering repeated backward movement as the reader tries to hold too many ideas in working memory at once.
target: regression_rate > 0.35 + line_reread_count > 4 → overloaded state

Consider a directed acyclic graph G=(V,E) where each vertex v∈V has an associated weight w(v)∈ℝ and each edge (u,v)∈E has a capacity c(u,v)>0. The minimum cut of G — defined as the partition of V into two disjoint sets S and T such that the source s∈S, the sink t∈T, and the sum of capacities of edges crossing from S to T is minimised — equals the maximum flow from s to t by the max-flow min-cut theorem. The Ford-Fulkerson algorithm computes this by iteratively finding augmenting paths in the residual graph G_f, where G_f contains a forward edge (u,v) with capacity c(u,v)−f(u,v) and a backward edge (v,u) with capacity f(u,v) for each edge (u,v)∈E with flow f(u,v).

The passage above introduces graph theory, set theory, optimisation theory, and algorithm theory simultaneously, each building on the previous. A reader without the prerequisite knowledge must hold definitions of G, V, E, w(v), c(u,v), S, T, s, t, f(u,v), and G_f in working memory while trying to understand the theorem. Working memory capacity is limited to approximately seven plus or minus two chunks. This paragraph exceeds that limit significantly, causing the reader to regress to the start of sentences they just read in order to refresh decaying memory traces before attempting to parse the next clause.

What TL;DR should do here: detect very high regression, very short saccades, highest line re-read count, classify as overloaded, and send the paragraph to the AI with mode=simplify. The response rewrites the content in plain language with bullet points. Use Alt+2 to trigger this.
